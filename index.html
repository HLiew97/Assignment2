What Is an Emotional Support Animal (ESA)?

An Emotional Support Animal (ESA) is a companion animal that provides therapeutic benefits to individuals with mental or emotional disabilities. Unlike service animals, ESAs do not require specialized training but offer vital emotional support to those who need it.

How to Qualify for an ESA

To legally recognize an animal as an ESA, an individual must obtain a letter from a licensed mental health professional (LMHP). This letter should:

  • Confirm a mental or emotional disability as defined by the DSM-5.
  • Explain how the ESA alleviates symptoms of the condition.
